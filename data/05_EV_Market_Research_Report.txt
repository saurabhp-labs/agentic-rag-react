ELECTRIC VEHICLE MARKET ANALYSIS REPORT 2024
============================================================

Prepared by: Global Market Research Institute

Report Date: October 2024 | Report ID: GMRI-EV-2024-Q3


Executive Summary
----------------------------------------

The global electric vehicle (EV) market reached $384.7 billion in 2023 and is projected to grow to $1.1 trillion by 2030, representing a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 17.8%. This report analyzes market trends, key players, regional dynamics, and future projections.

Key findings indicate that battery technology improvements, government incentives, and expanding charging infrastructure are the primary growth drivers. China remains the largest market, accounting for 59% of global EV sales in 2023.


================================================================================


Market Size and Growth
----------------------------------------

Global EV sales reached 14.2 million units in 2023, up from 10.5 million in 2022, representing 35% year-over-year growth. Battery electric vehicles (BEVs) accounted for 72% of sales, with plug-in hybrid electric vehicles (PHEVs) comprising the remaining 28%.

Regional breakdown of 2023 EV sales: China - 8.4 million units (59%), Europe - 3.2 million units (23%), North America - 1.7 million units (12%), Rest of World - 0.9 million units (6%).

The average selling price of EVs decreased by 8% in 2023 due to battery cost reductions and increased competition. Entry-level EV prices now start at $27,000 in the US market.


Battery Technology Trends
----------------------------------------

Lithium-ion battery pack costs fell to $139/kWh in 2023, down from $161/kWh in 2022. Industry experts project costs will reach $100/kWh by 2026, achieving price parity with internal combustion vehicles.

Solid-state battery technology is expected to enter commercial production by 2027. Companies like Toyota, QuantumScape, and Samsung SDI are leading development efforts with promised energy densities of 400+ Wh/kg.


================================================================================


Competitive Landscape
----------------------------------------

Top 10 EV manufacturers by 2023 global sales: 1. BYD (3.0M units), 2. Tesla (1.8M units), 3. Volkswagen Group (0.9M units), 4. SAIC-GM-Wuling (0.8M units), 5. Geely (0.7M units), 6. Stellantis (0.5M units), 7. BMW Group (0.5M units), 8. Hyundai Motor (0.4M units), 9. Mercedes-Benz (0.4M units), 10. Li Auto (0.4M units).

Tesla market share declined from 18% in 2022 to 13% in 2023 due to increased competition from Chinese manufacturers. BYD became the worlds largest EV manufacturer, surpassing Tesla in Q4 2023.


Charging Infrastructure
----------------------------------------

Global public charging points reached 2.7 million in 2023, a 40% increase from 2022. Fast charging stations (50kW+) grew by 55%, with ultra-fast chargers (150kW+) increasing by 80%.

The US has 168,000 public charging points across 64,000 locations. The Bipartisan Infrastructure Law allocates $7.5 billion for EV charging, targeting 500,000 chargers by 2030.


================================================================================


Regional Analysis: China
----------------------------------------

Chinas EV market is driven by strong government support, domestic manufacturing capabilities, and consumer acceptance. The NEV (New Energy Vehicle) penetration rate reached 35% of new car sales in 2023.

Key policies include purchase subsidies (phasing out in 2024), license plate preferences in major cities, and manufacturing incentives. Chinese battery manufacturers CATL and BYD control 54% of global battery production.


Regional Analysis: Europe
----------------------------------------

European EV adoption accelerated with the EU Fit for 55 package targeting 55% emissions reduction by 2030. Norway leads with 82% EV share of new sales, followed by Sweden (54%) and Netherlands (35%).

The EU will ban new ICE vehicle sales from 2035. Major automakers including Volkswagen, BMW, and Mercedes have committed to full electrification by 2030-2035.


================================================================================


Regional Analysis: North America
----------------------------------------

US EV sales grew 47% in 2023, reaching 1.4 million units. The Inflation Reduction Act provides up to $7,500 in tax credits for qualifying EVs with domestic content requirements.

California leads state adoption with 25% EV share, followed by Washington (15%) and Oregon (14%). Thirteen states have adopted Californias Advanced Clean Cars II regulation requiring 100% zero-emission vehicle sales by 2035.


Market Projections
----------------------------------------

Projected global EV sales: 2024 - 17.5 million units, 2025 - 21.2 million units, 2026 - 25.8 million units, 2027 - 31.4 million units, 2028 - 38.2 million units, 2029 - 46.5 million units, 2030 - 56.7 million units.

EV market penetration is expected to reach 45% of global new car sales by 2030 and 70% by 2035.


================================================================================


Investment Opportunities
----------------------------------------

Key investment areas identified: 1) Battery manufacturing and recycling, 2) Charging infrastructure deployment, 3) EV component suppliers (motors, power electronics), 4) Software and connected services, 5) Raw material mining and processing (lithium, cobalt, nickel).

Total announced EV-related investments through 2030 exceed $1.2 trillion globally, with $210 billion committed in North America alone.


Challenges and Risks
----------------------------------------

Primary challenges: Raw material supply constraints (lithium, cobalt shortages expected 2025-2027), grid capacity limitations, consumer range anxiety, used EV resale value uncertainty, and geopolitical risks affecting supply chains.


Methodology
----------------------------------------

This report synthesizes data from government agencies, industry associations, company filings, and proprietary surveys of 2,500 consumers and 150 industry executives across 12 countries.
